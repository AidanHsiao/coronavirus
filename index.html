Information on the coronavirus

The coronavirus outbreak has a lot of people very worried about their own safety, and rightfully so! According to a COVID-19 tracking website, more than 200,000 people have been diagnosed with the coronavirus. It's become so serious that some counties in California have been prohibited non-essential travel outside of people's homes. However, there are basic precautions you can take to protect yourself from this constantly spreading virus. According to the WHO (World Health Organization), some include:

Wash your hands constantly

The main way to stop the coronavirus from going into your system is to constantly wash your hands with soap and water. As oversaid as it is, it genuinely does help. The reason why it helps is because the coronavirus is mostly transferred from object to hand contact. With that gone, the chance of you getting the coronavirus is considerably lowered.

Stay at least 6 feet away from others

While the coronavirus can be transferred through object to hand contact, the coronavirus is actually an airborne virus as well. What this means is that bits of the virus cling on to respiratory droplets (large or small) that are made and expelled everytime you breathe. These droplets can last for hours in the air. By staying 6 feet away from people, you lower the risk of breathing in these droplets.

Avoid touching your face

Following up on what was said earlier, the coronavirus is transferred from object to hand, sure. However, the real problems arise when your hands touch your face, which is when the coronavirus really starts to enter your system and cause problems. The easiest way to prevent this, other than washing your hands, is to generally avoid touching your face, as the coronavirus will have next to no chance of actually entering your system, and will just stay on your hand.


And finally, just be aware of what's going on. While many people can make jokes about the coronavirus, the numbers are constantly rising. In where we live, the situation has already become very bad, to the point where non-essential travel outside of the house is forbidden. To learn more, the CDC have made a site detailing everything the average person needs to know about the coronavirus and how to stop its spread.
